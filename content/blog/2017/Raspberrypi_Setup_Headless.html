--- title: "setUp Raspberrypi headless" created: !!timestamp '2017-08-08 0:15:00' image: /media/images/blog/2017/raspberry-pi-logo.jpg tags: - Raspberrypi --- {% block excerpt %} {% mark excerpt %} [Raspberrypi](https://www.raspberrypi.org/) is a small computer, but it don't have screen.Here is how I configure my PI with a single ethernet cable, after this setup , you can login your PI using ssh from your computer. {% endmark %} {% endblock %} #What you need You will need a Raspberrypi, a SD card (and card reader) , an ethernet cable and a Wifi USB dongle #Flash Respbian on the SD card you can use [Etcher](https://etcher.io/) to burn [Resbian](https://www.raspberrypi.org/downloads/raspbian/) to your card. and after that done , Insert the SD card into your PI,you are ready to rock #Ssh into your PI with cable Power on your PI, connect the PI with your router with a normal ethernet cable , and after 1 minute , try execute below command from the computer that connect to the same router {% syntax shell %} ssh pi@raspberrypi.local (password:raspberrypi) {% endsyntax %} Bingo! your are in RPI now! #Config wifi Now let's config the wifi setting , so we can throw the ethernet cable away. in RPI add your wifi information in `/etc/wpa_supplicant/wpa_supplicant.conf` {% syntax shell %} network={ ssid="your-network-ssid-name" psk="your-network-password" } {% endsyntax %} Attach the wifi USB dongle and unplug the ethernet cable, and reboot the RPI(power off and then power on) and wait 1 minute , try `ssh pi@raspberrypi.local`. Now you should be able to login with wifi
